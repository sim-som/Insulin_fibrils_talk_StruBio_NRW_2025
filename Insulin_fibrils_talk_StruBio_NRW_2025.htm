Amyloid Diseases

Disorders caused by protein misfolding and aggregation into toxic amyloid fibrils:

Source: (Iadanza et al., 2018)

Amyloids and Neurodegeneration

  • 100+ years: Abnormal brain inclusions identified as disease hallmarks
  • Key proteins: Aβ, tau, α-synuclein, TDP-43 define major neurodegenerative diseases
    • Alzheimer's Disease (AD)
    • Parkinson's Disease (PD)
  • Genetics link: Mutations directly cause filament formation → Disease

The Cryo-EM Revolution for Amyloid Fibrils

Before 2017:

  • X-ray fiber diffraction: β-sheet rich structure
  • Solid-state NMR: Limited to in vitro samples
  • EM: Insufficient resolution for atomic modeling

The Breakthrough (2013-2017):

  • New electron detectors
  • computational hardware for image processing (e.g. JURECA: 23.5 petaflops)
  • Helical reconstruction algorithms
  • First brain-derived structures: Tau fibrils from Alzheimer's disease (Fitzpatrick et al., 2017)

Disease-Specific Amyloid Folds

Structure Defines Pathology

Protein Diseases Key Finding
Tau AD, CTE, Pick's, PSP, CBD Each disease has distinct fold
α-Synuclein PD, MSA, JOS MSA vs Lewy pathology folds
TDP-43 ALS, FTD subtypes FTLD-A vs FTLD-B folds
Amyloid-β Alzheimer's disease Type I vs Type II filaments

Polymorphism:
Given protein can adopt different fibril structures -> Define distinct diseases (Scheres et al., 2023)

The In Vitro Problem

A Critical Gap in Amyloid Research

  • Recombinant Aβ, tau, α-synuclein → Different fibril folds than patient derived fibrils
  • Limited disease relevance of most in vitro fibril structures
  • Need for patient-derived material -> More challenging structure determination
    • Sarkosyl based extraction -> often low fibril yield
    • Other non-relevant (?) structures in micrographs -> Difficult localization
    • Typically fewer polymorphs compared to in vitro
  • (We are also working on brain-extracted Aβ fibrils)
  • However ...

Insulin in vitro Amyloid Fibrils are Disease Relevant

  • Brain amyloids: Aggregation occurs in vivo → in vitro models inadequate

  • Insulin amyloids: Aggregation occurs in pharmaceutical preparations → in vitro models ARE the problem

  • Key hormone for glucose regulation and primary drug for diabetes treatment

  • Rising global demand (global type 2 diabetes pandemic (Bhutta et al., 2021) )

  • Aggregation in insulin vials and pens

  • Challenges in storage and handling, especially in low- and middle-income countries (Pendsey et al., 2023)

  • Direct injection of aggregated material -> injection site amyloidosis

Clinical Impact of Insulin Amyloidosis

Clinical Manifestations:

  • Injection site amyloidosis and inflammation
  • Unpredictable insulin absorption
  • Poor glycemic control
  • Patient adherence issues

Source: (Samlaska et al., 2020)

Structural Questions:

  • What are the atomic structures of pharmaceutical insulin amyloids?
  • Do different medical insulin formulations form different fibrils?
  • Can we design aggregation-resistant formulations?

Comparative Study Design

Two Insulin Systems, Different Clinical Relevance

System 1 - Recombinant Human Insulin (pH 2)

  • Model system for understanding basic aggregation
  • Less disease-relevant conditions
  • Baseline structural comparison

System 2 - Insulin Glargine (Medical)

  • Long-acting insulin analog widely used clinically
  • Aggregation under pharmaceutical conditions
  • Direct clinical relevance to injection site amyloidosis

Rationale: Compare structures to understand how formulation affects fibril fold

Sample Preparation Strategy

From Pharmaceutical Formulations to Cryo-EM Grids

System 1 - Human Insulin pH 2:

Based on established protocol (Thorlaksen et al., 2023)

  • Elevated temperature 60 °C
  • Agitation at 600 rpm
  • Maturation time: 1 day

System 2 - Insulin Glargine:

Abasaglar KwikPen (Eli Lilly) (pH 4)

  • Elevated temperature 60 °C
  • Agitation at 300 rpm
  • Maturation time: 1 day

Cryo-EM Data Collection

Microscopy Setup:

  • Titan Krios G4 300kV
  • TFS Falcon 4
  • 100k magnification, pixel size: 0.8 Å
  • Total Dose: 40 Åe/Å2

Data Collection Strategy:

  • 10k (human insulin) / 20k (glargine) micrographs
  • Defocus range: [−2,−0.7] μm
  • Automation via EPU (TFS)

Image Processing Workflow

From Raw Images to 3D Structure

Processing Pipeline:

  1. Motion correction& CTF estimation
  2. Particle picking(helical segments)
  3. 2D classification -> Separation of different polymorphs
  4. Initial map generation
  5. Helical reconstruction RELION (He & Scheres, 2017)
  6. 3D refinement & resolution assessment
  7. Model building & validation

Key Challenges:

  • (Low population of certain) polymorphs
  • Helical parameter determination
  • Initial map generation

Direct Comparison

Two Formulations, More than Two Distinct Folds

Standard free energy of amyloid formation (Eisenberg & McLachlan, 1986; Sawaya et al., 2021)

Direct Comparison

Two Formulations, More than Two Distinct Folds

Key Differences:

  • Trimer (human insulin) vs. dimer (glargine polymorphs)
  • Glargine more compact
  • Glargine polymorphs: Similar protofibril fold but different interfaces
  • Glargine fibrils more stable
  • Large "hydrophobic zipper" motif for Glargine slow twister

Disease Relevance Assessment

From Structure to Clinical Understanding

Human Insulin pH 2:

  • Model system insights
  • Novel structure

Insulin Glargine:

  • Direct clinical relevance
  • Real-world pharmaceutical conditions

Impact:

  • First atomic structures of pharmaceutical insulin amyloids
  • Medical insulin fibrils ≠ standard human insulin fibrils
  • Framework for analyzing further insulin formulations

Broader Implications

Connecting Structure to Function

Clinical Relevance:

  • Understanding injection site pathology
  • Informing insulin formulation design -> Reduced aggregation propensity
  • Potential diagnostic applications -> Amyloid specific markers

Future Directions

Next Steps in the Research

  • Structure determination of additional medical insulin variants
  • Design of analogs with reduced aggregation tendency
  • Mutated small hydrophobic residues in stabilizing regions to large polar residues
  • Polymorphism inherent problem -> Several different targets -> Time resolved cryo-EM

Future Directions: Time resolved cryo-EM

Future Directions: Time resolved cryo-EM

IAPP fibrils polymorph progression (Wilkinson et al., 2023)

  • Cryo-EM reconstruction at different time points of the aggregation
  • Already done for Tau and IAPP (Lövestam et al., 2023; Wilkinson et al., 2023)
  • Both could also show sequential formation: Monomer -> polymorph -> polymorphs -> polymorphs*
  • Maybe also one initial intermediate insulin fibril
  • Ideal target for anti-aggregation strategies
